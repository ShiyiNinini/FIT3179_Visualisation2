Where People Live vs Where Beds Are

An interactive analysis of population distribution and healthcare infrastructure across Malaysia (2015-2022)

Healthcare Infrastructure Overview

Key Observations

  • Uneven distribution: Urban states like Kuala Lumpur and Putrajaya have 2-3× more beds per capita than rural states
  • Improving trend: Overall bed availability increased from 1.8 to 2.1 per 1,000 people (2015-2022)
  • COVID-19 impact: Accelerated healthcare infrastructure investment visible in 2020-2022 period

Hospital Beds Distribution Analysis

This choropleth map visualizes the distribution of hospital beds per 1,000 people across Malaysian states. The color intensity represents the bed-to-population ratio, with darker red colors indicating higher bed availability.

Interactive Features:

Use the year slider to explore how this distribution has changed over time from 2015 to 2022.

What This Reveals:

  • Federal territories maintain highest bed ratios (2.5-2.8 per 1,000)
  • East Malaysian states lag behind West Malaysia
  • Rural states like Perlis and Kedah show slower improvement rates

Population Demographics Analysis

65% Young & Middle-Aged Malaysia's population is predominantly aged 15-59, creating significant demand for diverse healthcare services

Understanding Malaysia's Demographic Landscape

Malaysia's diverse population requires careful healthcare planning to address varying needs across different age groups and ethnic communities.

Age Distribution Insights:

  • Selangor & Johor lead: Largest populations concentrated in economically active states
  • Aging population trend: Seniors (60+) increasing from 9% to 11% (2015-2022)

Ethnic Composition Patterns:

  • Regional diversity: Sabah and Sarawak show unique ethnic compositions compared to peninsula
  • Cultural considerations: Multi-ethnic population necessitates culturally sensitive healthcare delivery

Healthcare Preparedness for Aging Population

Key Finding: Aging Rural Areas Under-Resourced

  • Negative correlation: States with higher elderly populations tend to have fewer hospital beds per capita
  • Urban-rural divide: Young people migrate to cities, leaving rural areas with aging populations and limited healthcare

Understanding the Healthcare

This scatter plot reveals a concerning pattern: states with higher proportions of elderly residents often have lower hospital bed availability, creating a mismatch between need and resources.

Critical Observations:

  • Perlis: Highest aging rate (11.5%) with limited beds (1.75 per 1,000)
  • Sarawak: East Malaysia's aging challenge - 12% elderly but only 1.7 beds
  • KL advantage: Balanced aging (11%) with strong infrastructure (2.5 beds)

Interactive Features:

Use the year slider to observe how this relationship has evolved from 2015 to 2022. Notice which states are closing the gap and which are falling further behind.

Regional Trends and Comparisons

Trend Observations

  • Positive trajectory: Hospital bed growth (+18%) outpaced population growth (+12.5%) from 2015-2022
  • COVID-19 catalyst: Sharp increase in healthcare infrastructure investment visible post-2020
  • Regional disparity persists: West Malaysia maintains 3-4 times more beds than East Malaysia in absolute numbers

Why Regional Analysis Matters

Understanding regional differences in population growth and healthcare infrastructure is critical for equitable healthcare planning and resource allocation across Malaysia's diverse geography.

National Trends:

  • Population grew steadily from 31M to 33.5M
  • Hospital beds increased from 49K to 52K
  • Beds-per-capita ratio improved from 1.8 to 2.1

East vs West Dynamics:

  • Population ratio: West Malaysia houses ~80% of total population
  • Infrastructure gap: East Malaysia's bed ratio lags at 1.5 vs 2.2 in West
  • Growth rates: Both regions show parallel improvement trends

Policy Implications:

Targeted investment in East Malaysian healthcare infrastructure is needed to achieve national healthcare equity goals.
